How to Choose the Right Marathon Shoe

By Staff • Published: February 10, 2026

This guide covers stack height, drop, and foam density. Picking footwear can reduce risk of injury during training. The right fit helps alignment. While this is a health topic, our clinic is focused on teeth, so consider this general wellness advice rather than medical care. If you grind teeth during training we can help with night guards.

Footwear basics

Stack height affects cushioning. Drop changes how the foot lands. Foam density shapes feel. There is no perfect shoe for all people. Try several models. Rotate pairs to vary loading. Learn more.

Common mistakes

  • Buying based on color instead of fit
  • Skipping a break in period
  • Changing drop too fast

When shoes cause mouth guards

If training stress leads to clenching, a night guard might help. See our care pages.

What to Do If You Chip a Tooth

By Staff • Published: January 12, 2025

Save any pieces if possible. Rinse gently. Avoid very hot or cold food. Wax can cover sharp edges for a short time. Call for a visit. If pain is severe, we will try to see you the same day.

Short term steps

  1. Rinse the mouth with warm water
  2. Use temporary dental wax if sharp
  3. Call to schedule a visit

What to expect at the visit

We will review the tooth and look for cracks. Some cases need smoothing only. Others need a filling or a crown. Care instructions are clear and simple.

For crowns, see same day crowns. For general info, click here.

Nutrition on Technical Trails

By Staff • Published: June 2, 2025

Hydration matters. Steady carbs keep energy even. If gels upset the stomach, try small bites of real food. A calm pace helps digestion.

Options to try

Use a simple plan for long runs. Alternate water and electrolyte drinks. Small salty snacks may help on hot days. Test your plan in training.

Extra tips

  • Practice eating while moving
  • Pick simple foods
  • Adjust for heat
